Feature 05

Technical FAQ

What is the difference between the 3 Chat Models?

Finder utilizes three distinct, specialized AI models. Engineer is optimized for finding and recommending your adsorber when you have specific custom applications or dimensions that aren't covered in Guided Mode. Explorer is for general questions (e.g., why is my adsorber turning orange, or asking it what the equivalent of a Vario is to our new modern adsorbers). Axis is an internal mode used for deep research, projections, specialized questions, and strategic planning.

How does the Guided Mode calculate the right adsorber?

The Guided Mode uses a deterministic constraint engine. It takes your physical machine inputs—such as headspace volume, oil capacity, and operating temperature—and calculates the precise airflow and moisture capacity needed, filtering the catalog to only show mechanically compatible units.

When should I use Guided Flow vs. Chat Mode?

Guided Flow is a step-by-step, calculator-driven constraint engine ideal when you have specific parameter inputs and need a direct, deterministic hardware recommendation. Chat Mode is designed for open-ended exploration, troubleshooting edge cases, querying internal documentation, and designing custom setups without strict numerical bounds.

Can I save and share my calculation results?

Yes. Every configured system, chat session, and tier comparison generates a unique state that can be instantly captured. You can export detailed PDF technical reports or share direct links with your engineering team to review the exact specifications and reasoning that led to a specific adsorber recommendation.
